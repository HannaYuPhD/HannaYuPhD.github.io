Childfree Lifestyle

1. A Mixed-Methods Study of Childfree Dual-Income Couples

(PIs: Hanna Yu & Čante Nakanishi)

This study is supported by the Family Social Science Small Grant ($1,500). This project focuses on the lives of childfree individuals in committed relationships. Our research team explores their relationships, well-being, health, financial management, and future expectations. This study aims to understand the lived experiences, strengths, and needs of childfree individuals.

Research Team: Michelle Pasco, Ph.D., Virginia Solis Zuiker, Ph.D., and Aylin Ramirez (Undergraduate Student)

2. From Family Experiences to Childfree: Exploring the Decision Not to Have Children in Young Adults Using Supervised Machine Learning

(Working Paper)

This study examines how comprehensive family experiences during adolescence may predict childfree intentions in young adulthood using machine learning. The contribution of this study is to identify which factors in family dynamics are quantitatively related to childfree intentions. Additionally, it demonstrates how to predict a rare event (the choice to remain childfree) in social science using machine learning models.

3. Exploring Reasons for Being a Childfree Couple

(Working Paper)

This study investigates how couples decide to become childfree, acknowledging that this choice is often a joint decision rather than an individual one. It contributes to the field through dyadic couple interviews, exploring how both partners' perspectives shape the final decision to remain childfree.
