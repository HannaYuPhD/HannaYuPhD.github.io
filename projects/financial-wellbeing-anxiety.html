Financial Management and Health

Study 1. Financial Dynamics in Newlywed Couples: Dyadic Patterns, Predictors, and Health Implications

This study focuses on newlywed couples and their dyadic financial dynamics. First, I will identify distinct patterns of couples' financial dynamics based on reports from both partners. Next, I will examine which financial, relational, and psychological factors best differentiate these patterns. Finally, I will assess whether demographic characteristics and subsequent depression and health outcomes vary across these dyadic patterns.

Study 2. Financial Capability and Long-Term Health: A 10-Year Prediction of Self-Rated and Chronic Health Outcomes

This study focuses on individuals and the long-term health implications of financial capability. Specifically, I will evaluate how distinct dimensions of financial capability predict self-rated health and chronic health outcomes 10 years later. I will also identify which components of financial capability are most influential in shaping these long-term health outcomes.

Taken together, these two studies will identify effective financial management practices among newlywed couples and clarify how financial dynamics and financial capability relate to long-term health. The findings will inform screening and prevention strategies for researchers, practitioners, educators, and policymakers, with the goal of strengthening financial management at both the couple and individual levels, improving health, and reinforcing the evidence base for financial education as a universal prevention strategy for health.
